Remote Sensing Analysis of Woody Species Succession in the Carden Alvar

Using Landsat imagery, woody species succession was assessed and displayed in an interactive web map to indicate priority areas for restoration efforts.

Project Abstract


Carden Alvar is an extensive landscape consisting of multiple types of alvar communities, including pavement alvars which are being threatened by woody species encroachment. This could cause a shift into a shrub or forest alvar which could be detrimental for the many unique, and possibly threatened or endangered species that thrive in pavement alvars. The Nature Conservancy of Canada (NCC) would like to protect and restore the pavement alvars on their properties by creating a time series going back approximately 50 years

With the use of satellite imagery, an analysis of where woody species encroachment is occurring can be determined using GIS software such as CATALYST and ArcGIS Pro. Highlighting existing species in the impacted areas utilizing species data from both NCC and open-source resources, will help the organization identify if there has been a shift in the alvar composition. Threatened or endangered species will also be identifiable. Combining this data, an analysis of priority restoration areas can be achieved, and an ArcGIS Online web map will be created for NCC staff to assist in the decision-making process of whether restoration should take place or if encroachment in some areas is appropriate
